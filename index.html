A. Glycosuria

The excretion of glucose into the urine, which can lead to dehydration.

B. Albuminuria

A condition that occurs when the kidneys are unable to properly filter substances, especially proteins.

C. Kidney Stones

A disease caused by the formation of deposits in the renal pelvis or urinary bladder.

D. Diabetes Mellitus

Type 1 Diabetes: Caused by genetic factors and usually appears during childhood.
Type 2 Diabetes: Caused by excessive consumption of sugar and fat, generally occurs in adults.

E. Diabetes Insipidus

A disease characterized by the production of large amounts of dilute urine.

F. Kidney Failure

Caused by damage to the glomeruli, resulting in the inability to carry out the filtration process.

G. Uremia

A toxic condition caused by the accumulation of waste products in the blood due to kidney failure.

H. Nephritis

Inflammation of the nephrons in the kidneys, which can be caused by bacterial infection.

I. Polyuria

A disorder characterized by an increased frequency of urination, often associated with excessive thirst.
